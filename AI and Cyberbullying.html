AI and Cyberbullying: How Social Media Detects and Fails to Prevent Harassment

Welcome to our exploration of the critical topic of AI's role in detecting and combating cyberbullying on social media platforms. As technology advances, the use of artificial intelligence (AI) has been integrated into social media platforms to help prevent harassment and protect users from harmful behavior. However, AI is not without its limitations, and social media platforms still struggle to fully prevent bullying and harassment.

How AI algorithms identify harmful content

The system relies on the detection of cyberbullying text along with the themes/categories associated with cyberbullying such as racist, sexual, physical mean, swear and other, using support vector machines and Logistic regression

Why AI detection isn't enough to stop cyberbullying?

How does human intervention still play a role?
AI detection can't fully stop cyberbullying due to context, slang, evolving language.

AI can generate false positives or false negatives and lack the ability to understand emotional impact or cultural nuances.

Human intervention is important as humans understand the situations better, provide support, and show care, especially across different cultures.

Real world examples
☞ Creating fake social media profiles, fake audio or videos, offensive memes, or ‘deepfake’ pornography

☞ Bombarding the victim with nasty automated messages or comments

☞ ‘Sextortion’, where a perpetrator threatens to release AI-generated ‘nudes’ unless the victim hands over money or real intimate photos

☞ Sending mean texts or emails (hurtful or threatening messages)

☞ Spread rumors

☞ Intention to harm an individual

How to protect your account ?
Account privacy :

Change your privacy settings on social media to limit who can see your posts and contact you

Block and Mute :

Use the block and mute features to prevent harassers from contacting you or seeing your content

Friends and Family :

Talk to trusted friends or family members about what you're experiencing.

Stay safe, informed and aware

Fake news creates confusion, undermines trust in legitimate sources, and leads to misunderstandings about important social and political issues. Misleading stories about medical treatments or diseases may lead people to make misinformed decisions about their health.

How to identify Fake News :
  1. Check the source

  2. Check the author

  3. Maintain a critical mindset

  4. Check the facts

  5. Check the comments

  6. Check images are authentic

  7. Use a fact-checking site

How to recognize Scams :
Pressure you to act immediately :

Scammers create urgency to make you act quickly without thinking.

Claims of a problem or prize :

Scammers might say there’s an issue with your account or that you’ve won a prize, and ask you to verify personal information.

How to Secure your account :
  • Do a security checkup

  • Update software

  • Use strong, unique passwords

  • Remove unnecessary apps and extensions

  • Be cautious with suspicious messages and content

AI can spot harmful content on social media, but it often misunderstands the full meaning, such as context or emotions. It can't completely stop bullying because it sometimes makes mistakes. People are still needed to make sure things are handled correctly, offer support, and understand different cultural perspectives, showing that both AI and humans are needed to fight cyberbullying effectively.
